Dyloo

Website redesign to communicate clearly and effectively

My Role

UX & UI Designer

Timeline

3 weeks

Deliverable

Landing page

Status

Live

About Dyloo

Dyloo is a web platform that allows people in the professional field to create digital business cards as a networking tool, expressing their professional identity with pre-designed templates.

Context

I carried out this project during my work as a UX/UI Designer at Dilvant. The digital product Dyloo was being developed, but in order to launch it to the market, a website was needed, so the client coordinated the creation of a website with another agency. When the design was delivered to our team, upon review we noticed that the proposal did not follow the design guidelines established in the Design System previously defined, nor did it meet the heuristics necessary to provide a good user experience. The content presented was not aligned with the product MVP nor the business objectives for the initial stage.

Solution

Because of this, and because I had been part of the team that designed the digital product, I was asked to create a new website proposal considering a modern and minimalist design that would align with client and user expectations.

Strategy & Scope

Who is the user?

  • Professional people with little knowledge about digital cards but familiar with technology
  • People who need to share their information easily and quickly
  • People who like to try new and free products
  • People looking to grow professionally

What do they want to achieve?

  • Learn about the service/product that Dyloo provides
  • Know how Dyloo would benefit them
  • Reduce doubts about the product to acquire it
  • Register to create their personal profile

What does the website need to deliver?

  • Present the company, who we are, how Dyloo works, plans-prices and their benefits
  • Present the most frequent questions to clear up doubts and contact alternatives
  • Invite users to register to acquire their Dyloo card

Design Evaluation

Heuristic Evaluation

Since there was already a design elaborated, we chose to conduct a heuristic evaluation based on Susan Weinschenk's heuristics matrix in order to find opportunities for improvement in terms of usability.

Evaluation team: Areli Cadenillas (Product Designer), Joscelyn Vásquez (UX & UI Designer), Nicole Sierra (UX & UI Designer)

Main points for improvement identified:

  • Inconsistency in flows, information presentation and visual elements
  • User attention may be affected due to the absence of visual breathing and excess of proposed animations
  • The content presented is not consistent with the product to be offered

Improvements Applied

Information Architecture

After reviewing the available information about the product and since it was an MVP, it was viable to design and implement a landing page as a starting point to introduce the product, its features and benefits. A new organization and content hierarchy was established.

Sketching & Wireframing

Based on the information obtained, analyzed and defined, I elaborated sketches which I then converted to medium-fidelity wireframes. After clarifying the idea, I proceeded to high-fidelity design considering brand identity and the design system developed during Dyloo digital product creation.

Animations in Figma

To give more dynamism to the interface for a landing page and to facilitate content reading during scrolling, animations were proposed for certain UI elements. Since we didn't have After Effects for simulation, I simulated animations using Figma's prototyping option, making respective indications to the development team.

Key Learning

Design Ownership

It was my first project taking responsibility in interface design decisions. I consulted with more experienced people and their suggestions allowed me to achieve an efficient and understandable design delivery for developers.

Animation Specifications

First time making specifications regarding animation use in the interface to give dynamism and ease of reading content. Animations were made in Figma with annotations for better developer communication.

Design Principles Applied

📐 Consistency

All visual elements followed the established Design System, maintaining consistency across all pages.

🎯 Clarity

Content hierarchy ensured users understood the product value without cognitive overload.

✨ Modernism

Clean, minimalist design with strategic animation to enhance readability and user engagement.

📱 Responsiveness

Design adapted seamlessly from desktop to mobile while maintaining visual integrity.

Reflection

This was my first project taking responsibility in the decisions at the interface design level. I realized the importance of consulting with more experienced people in the field as mentors, and thanks to their suggestions I was able to achieve an efficient and understandable design delivery for developers.

It was also the first time I made specifications regarding animation use in the interface to give more dynamism and ease of reading content. These animations were made in Figma with annotations for better communication with developers.
