Mibanco | Variable Compensation Simulator

Design and validation: from execution to discovery

My Role

UX Specialist & UI Designer

Timeline

16 weeks

Tools

Figma, Figjam, MS Office

Status

In production

Context

During my work as UX Specialist at Mibanco, the team received the requirement to redesign the Variable Compensation Simulator, originally developed in Excel. The objective was to migrate it to a web platform that would facilitate its use and visualization. The assignment came with a defined scope, so the initial focus was purely on visual and functional design, without a prior research phase.

What is the Variable Compensation Simulator? A tool for business advisors that helps them know their business goals for the month to obtain an additional bonus to their salary based on their level of achievement.

Although I began the project with an assignment to do visual redesign, I ended up discovering opportunities for trust and transparency that allowed me to propose recommendations to improve the user experience of business advisors.

Design Process

I considered two main stages: a first one focused on adapting the existing tool to the digital environment, and a second one oriented toward validation with end users in real context.

Phase 1: Design Based on Requirements

In this phase, I sought to understand the functional logic of the RV Simulator in its original version (for macros in Excel) and transform it into a clear, efficient digital experience aligned with business objectives.

Stakeholder mapping: Identified key stakeholders for reviews and validations across various areas.

Analysis of the main tool: Organized understanding sessions with IT and analysts to understand technical connections and logistics. Mapped initial needs from business advisors' perspective.

Non-negotiable functionalities from a business perspective:

  • Responsive web platform: Mobile access to reduce computer dependence and accelerate daily planning
  • Self-managed credentials (login): Secure, self-managed access
  • Unfocus visualization: Traceability visualization to identify result variations and strengthen tool confidence

Web interface design: Focused on assembling user flows to define happy paths and alternative scenarios, defined information architecture, created UI Kit and elaborated wireframes. Collected feedback from peers and potential end users for iteration.

Phase 2: Usability Testing in Real Context

After implementation and ready for soft launch, I decided to conduct usability testing in the field with business advisors to observe how they used the tool outside the controlled design environment. What began as technical validation became a discovery stage revealing real use conditions, trust perceptions and unexpected needs.

Methodology:

  • 5 business advisors with 2-15 years experience
  • Metrics: Perceived ease, CSAT, and SUS
  • Duration: 40-50 minutes per session, moderated and in-person
  • Tasks: Email identification, login, interface exploration, simulation and result interpretation

Key metrics achieved:

93% Success Rate

Task completion on both mobile and desktop

4.5/5 Ease of Use

With opportunities in the simulation process

95.8/100 SUS

The new design rated as highly usable

Key Findings

📱 Web version improved accessibility

Advisors valued using it from their phone. This facilitated field access and increased product professionalism perception.

📊 Visual feedback increased understanding

Visual indicators helped advisors understand performance without mental calculations.

🔒 Doubts about data reliability

Several advisors expressed uncertainty about whether the new simulator reflected the same information as the Excel tool, affecting precision perception.

⚙️ Hardware limitations affected usability

Some advisors shared computers with low-resolution monitors, reducing legibility due to low contrast.

Recommendations

🧩 Design

Improve legibility and accessibility ensuring adequate contrast and visual clarity. Continue user research to optimize experience and better understand usage context.

💬 Communication

Implement a clear communication strategy that informs advisors about simulator updates and data sources, reinforcing trust and reducing confusion with the Excel version.

🎯 Behavior

Incorporate elements that promote understanding and motivation, such as progress indicators, metrics traceability and simple gamification components.

Reflection

This project allowed me to corroborate that a well-designed solution can become limited if it does not align with real use conditions and the perception of trust of its users. Field testing allowed me to discover needs not contemplated in the initial requirement, such as achievement traceability and data source visibility.

Beyond visual redesign, this exercise reinforced a key idea: Designing reliable products involves both functional clarity (easy and useful simulation) and operational transparency (real-time visibility of goal achievement).
