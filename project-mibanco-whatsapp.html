Mibanco por WhatsApp

Designing banking as a conversation

My Role

Conversational Product Designer

Timeline

Aug 2025 – Present

Team

Product, Data Science, AI, Business

Methods

Conversational design · Scenario mapping · Voice and tone · Iterative usability testing

Context

Mibanco por WhatsApp is a conversational channel designed to offer a simple, natural and close banking experience, aligned with the digital habits of Peruvian microentrepreneurs (97% of them use WhatsApp daily). The starting point was a concrete question: how to bring traditionally complex financial services to people who already use WhatsApp every day? The answer was not to build an app or replicate homebanking. It was to design banking as a conversation.

The Sami project at Mibanco resonated with the interest of key stakeholders, so it was proposed to take conversational banking to the next level, enhanced with artificial intelligence to promote self-management and reduce operational friction.

My Role

I was the first conversational designer for the channel and responsible for designing 4 end-to-end functionalities: flow definition, scenario mapping and interaction writing. I also established the initial voice and tone that laid the foundation for the conversational personality of Mibanco por WhatsApp.

This project represented an important leap in my practice: although I had taken my first steps in conversational design with Sami, Mibanco por WhatsApp demanded I take it to another level of complexity and scale. As the product grew, three colleagues joined the team: Leslie Hugo, Marco Hernando and Luis Tapia; with whom we built a very collaborative dynamic. Working with people from different trajectories and experiences enriched both the product and my own way of designing.

The Design Challenge

Designing for WhatsApp involves constraints that don't exist on other channels: no screens, no forms, no visual navigation. The interface is the conversation itself, and every word has real consequences on the user experience.

The additional challenge was the user: Peruvian microentrepreneurs with different levels of digitality, distrust toward digital financial channels and little time available.

Design Principles

🙌 Closeness and familiarity

WhatsApp as a familiar space reduces friction and cognitive load from first contact.

💡 Clarity and progressive guidance

One question at a time, at the right moment, accompanying the user without overloading them.

🎮 Sense of control

The user can inquire, simulate, advance or stop at their own pace.

✨ AI as facilitator

It interprets intentions, adapts the flow and escalates to a human advisor when needed, without exposing technological complexity.

Product in Constant Evolution

Mibanco por WhatsApp is operating with an average of ~2k conversations per day. We are in constant search for improvements, with the design team maintaining an active practice of follow-up and iteration organized around four fronts:

📊 Monitoring of metrics and funnels

We monitor behavior within each conversational flow: where users advance, where they stop and where they abandon.

📞 Calls with customers

We talk directly with users to understand behaviors that data doesn't explain.

🧪 Interviews and usability testing

We plan and execute sessions with users to evaluate specific flows before and after changes, to make decisions before implementing.

🔍 Analysis of external products

We review how other conversational channels solve similar problems, so we don't start from scratch.

Reflection

Designing for conversation forced me to keep thinking differently. Without screens or visual elements, design focuses on language clarity. This project continues to be one of the most challenging and enriching of my career, motivating me to explore new tools, ask for mentoring and review articles and books on the subject.
