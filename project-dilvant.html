Dilvant

Corporate website redesign to improve content clarity and navigation

My Role

Brand Designer, UX & UI Designer

Timeline

6 weeks

Deliverable

Website, Branding

Status

Live and implemented

Challenge

The company's objective for Q1 2023 was to relaunch its brand to start offering services to SMEs that wanted to use technology to transform their businesses. Previously, the company had a website that did not communicate the services it provided in an understandable way. During the web evaluation, it was evident that there was visual and content inconsistency, so I was asked to define the brand of the company and redesign the page.

Solution Approach

I conducted research to define the company's brand territory, values and with that propose the new brand identity of Dilvant. I approached desktop research to have references of visual layout from leading companies and analyze how they organized content, which audience they targeted, and what their navigation was like. I maintained constant communication with the development team in order to design responsive interfaces that are feasible at the development level and avoid rework.

Objectives

✓ Relaunch Dilvant's website to the American and Peruvian markets in Q1

✓ Consider the definition and alignment of corporate identity throughout the website to communicate the company's values and services

✓ Attract potential customers through the website by making navigation fluid and design simple

Branding Process

Research Phase:

  • Desk research: Research and define brand territory considering services and benefits offered
  • Internal analysis: Support from customer service area to understand company perception; analysis of team qualities
  • Competitive analysis: Comparative analysis of competitors using the color wheel to map positioning

Brand Definition: Dilvant wanted to see itself with a solid and stable image in front of its clients since they wanted to convey quality, trust and efficiency. We chose a flat base to emphasize stability and consistency. The V detail color changed to a deeper, more modern blue, and the effect went from beveled to flat—resulting in a fresher, more modern brand image.

Website Redesign Process

1. Understanding the Initial Context

Initial information architecture analysis: Built the initial IA to understand how content was organized and if it maintained consistency with the audience and use context. Identified opportunities for improvement in content organization and distribution.

Visual consistency and navigation review: Initially, the company had 6 pages: Home, About Us, Services, Blog, Join Us and Contact. During analysis, identified inconsistencies in content organization, element hierarchy and navigation difficulties.

Competitive and reference analysis: Reviewed layout and content from other websites, evidencing patterns to highlight trust, seriousness and quality of service. They had modern and fresh design indicating they are at the forefront of the industry.

2. Finding the Best Alternative

Sitemap: Established a new preliminary information architecture based on research and business objectives, validated with the area leader and reviewed iteratively based on client objectives and target audience.

User flows: Identified main tasks that final users would perform within the website and presented scenarios and happy paths for each one.

Sketching and wireframing: Sketched main screens by hand, converted to medium-fidelity wireframes, and presented to the area leader. After structure was confirmed, proceeded with final designs.

3. Delivering the Design

Responsive design: Considered responsive design for desktop, tablet and mobile with defined breakpoints: Desktop large (1920px), medium (1440px), small (1200px), Tablet large (992px), small (768px), Mobile large (600px), small (360px).

Screen flows & Hand-off: Conducted screen flows with annotations for developers. Design delivery had about three iterations due to evolving service list and the importance of development team communication for feasibility.

Visual QA: Reviewed styles, dimensions and content in test space using Google's DevTools, ensuring correct implementation in development. Once observations were addressed, the page was launched.

Key Learnings

Constant Communication is Key

Unlike other projects, constant communication between team members to evaluate design feasibility at development level allowed for smoother design delivery without friction.

Ownership & Responsibility

Having knowledge in Interactive Media Design allowed me to be given responsibility to build the brand and give it a new look & feel.

What I'd Do Differently

Consider guerrilla testing for usability improvements and implement SEO research and strategy, as positioning in web browsers is important for service-based websites.
