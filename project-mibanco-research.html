Mibanco | Understanding Adoption Barriers

Digital adoption barriers in customer service for traditional clients

My Role

UX Specialist

Timeline

Jun 2024 – Mar 2025

Methods

In-depth interviews · Surveys · Data analysis

Deliverables

User Persona · Journey Map · Research Insights

Context

Mibanco operated a centralized portfolio management model through WhatsApp and mailing as an alternative to in-person service. The product had more than 2,000 active clients, 95% retention and an NPS of 44. However, the digital re-engagement rate was low and there was little clarity about which client had the greatest potential for digital adoption and what prevented them from converting.

Key question: Who is the customer willing to be managed digitally? What barriers prevent them from adopting the channel? What does Sami need to be a truly valuable channel?

My Role

I led the research process from start to finish during three consecutive phases: from defining objectives and methodological design to analysis, synthesis and presentation of findings to product, business and data teams. The generated insights directly informed design decisions, backlog prioritization and segmentation strategy.

Research Process

The research was developed in three articulated phases over a year, each building on the findings of the previous one.

Phase 1: Traditional Clients (Jun 2024)

Question: Who is our customer and how do they perceive digital service?

Methodology: In-depth interviews (remote), 45–60 min per session

Sample: 10 customers from the traditional segment, ages 26-73, tenure 1-15 years

Key findings:

  • All 10 identified their in-person advisor as the main reference. None recognized Sami as their current advisor.
  • 7 out of 10 had not used the mobile app due to lack of knowledge or not seeing it as necessary.
  • 6 out of 10 expressed distrust by not knowing who was behind the WhatsApp messages.
  • Sami was perceived as an automated campaign bot, useful for quick queries but insufficient for complex issues.
  • Customers viewed digital service as a complement, not a substitute for the human advisor.

Phase 2: Early Adopters (Sep 2024)

Question: Who has greater predisposition to adopt digital service?

Methodology: Individual interviews + Quantitative analysis of interaction data, NPS and credit behavior

Sample: 15 active customers (10 heavy users / 5 light users), ages 25–48

Key findings:

  • The Early Adopter has a clear profile: under 29 years old, independent, commerce or services sector, less than 2 years tenure, punctual payer with established digital habit.
  • Seeks self-management without depending on the advisor, real-time support and ability to be served while doing other activities.
  • Main barriers: distrust due to digital scams, fear of investing time without immediate benefit and notification overload.

Phase 3: Re-engagement Analysis (Mar 2025)

Question: Why doesn't even the customer with a digital profile convert?

Methodology: Segmented remote interviews + Conversion funnel analysis + Interaction log analysis

Sample: 15 customers with digital re-engagement campaign, segmented into 4 behavioral groups

Key Findings

🎯 Adoption is not an attitude problem

It's a trust and context problem. Digitally aware customers will use the channel when they understand what they're using, trust who's contacting them, and perceive a concrete benefit.

📱 Sami has a positioning problem

Most perceive it as a campaign channel, not as an assistant capable of solving real queries. This limits its potential even among early adopters.

🎨 Re-engagement barriers are mostly design-related

Confusing forms, flow interruptions and lack of clarity in navigation explain a significant part of abandonment, not the user's lack of willingness.

👥 Actionable Early Adopter Profile

Young, independent, multitasker, with good payment history and digital habit. Can be segmented with concrete criteria from internal and external data.

Impact on Product

The insights from this research resulted in 6 concrete lines of action for the team:

Action: Refinement of Early Adopter segmentation criteria Area: Data + Business
Action: Update of User Persona and Journey Map by profile Area: Design
Action: Improvement of Sami navigation: nodes and knowledge base Area: Product
Action: Creation of onboarding flow to digital model Area: Design + Product
Action: Optimization of relationship journeys by profile Area: Design + Business
Action: Review of digital evaluation process (form) Area: Product + Technology

Reflection

This project allowed me to work research as a continuous and cumulative practice, where each phase refined both the questions and the answers. The greatest challenge was translating complex qualitative findings into actionable criteria for teams with different languages and priorities. The combination of qualitative methods with data analysis was key to giving robustness to the insights and generating confidence in the recommendations.
